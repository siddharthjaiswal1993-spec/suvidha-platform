# Personas and Jobs-to-be-Done

All names, ages, and biographical details below are synthetic composites created for design
purposes and do not represent real individuals. The eight golden/demo flows referenced in each
persona's "Golden flows touched" line are: (1) Unified onboarding, (2) Address-change life event,
(3) Nomination readiness, (4) Mobile-number change, (5) Tax notice/grievance orchestration,
(6) Family assisted access, (7) Post-death succession, (8) Institution ops console processing.

---

## 1. Independent Citizen

**Role.** A working adult managing their own institutional relationships without routine help from
anyone else.

**Profile sketch.** Ananya Deshmukh, 34, a product manager at a mid-size firm in Pune. Renting an
apartment, married, no children yet, comfortable with apps and online banking, holds accounts at
two banks, one mutual fund platform, one insurer, and the standard set of government identity
documents. Moved cities twice in the last six years for work.

**Goals**
- See every institutional relationship she holds in one place, with current status.
- Know immediately when an address, mobile number, or nominee record has drifted out of sync.
- Complete routine administrative actions (renewals, updates) without repeated data entry.
- Track every request she has open with any institution until it is actually resolved.

**JTBD statements**
- When I move to a new address, I want to update it once and see every institution that still has
  my old one, so I can complete every required change without hunting down each portal myself.
- When I open the app after a few weeks away, I want to immediately see anything overdue or
  requiring my attention, so I can avoid missing a renewal or a deadline.
- When I submit a request to any institution, I want to track its real status in the same place
  I track everything else, so I don't have to remember which portal to check.

**Pain points**
- No single view of which institutions hold outdated information about her.
- Re-entering the same proof of address or identity for every new relationship.
- No reminder system that spans more than one institution's renewal calendar.

**Golden flows touched.** 1 (Unified onboarding), 2 (Address-change), 3 (Nomination readiness),
4 (Mobile-number change), 5 (Tax notice/grievance).

## 2. Family Administrator

**Role.** A family member who helps a parent, spouse, or dependant with permitted administrative
work, without holding legal authority over them.

**Profile sketch.** Rohit Verma, 41, an IT manager in Bengaluru helping his 68-year-old father in
Lucknow keep his bank, pension, and insurance records current after his mother's recent surgery
left his father managing the household's paperwork alone for the first time.

**Goals**
- See what his father has explicitly permitted him to view or assist with — nothing more.
- Help complete specific tasks (a renewal, a form, an address update) without needing power of
  attorney for routine matters.
- Get notified when something in his father's institutional relationships needs attention.
- Never be mistaken, by the platform or by an institution, for having authority he doesn't have.

**JTBD statements**
- When my father asks for help with a renewal, I want to act within exactly the permission tier he
  granted me, so I never overstep what he's actually authorised.
- When something in my father's accounts needs urgent attention and he's unavailable, I want to be
  notified within my granted access scope, so the family doesn't miss a deadline.
- When I complete a delegated task, I want my father to see clearly what I did and when, so trust
  in the arrangement is easy to maintain.

**Pain points**
- Institutions today have no consistent way to recognise "helping family member" short of a full
  power of attorney, which is disproportionate for routine tasks.
- No consolidated way to see what he's been asked to help with versus what remains his father's
  alone.
- Risk of the family's assistance being mistaken for, or drifting into, unauthorised access.

**Golden flows touched.** 6 (Family assisted access), 2 (Address-change), 3 (Nomination
readiness), 4 (Mobile-number change).

## 3. Assisted Citizen

**Role.** An older adult, a person with a disability, or a low-digital-literacy user who delegates
limited assistance to a Family Administrator or Professional Representative rather than acting
alone.

**Profile sketch.** Kamlesh Yadav, 71, a retired schoolteacher in Kanpur, comfortable on the phone
but not confident navigating web forms or reading dense notices; relies on his daughter (a Family
Administrator) for anything requiring a portal or an app.

**Goals**
- Stay informed, in plain language, about anything that needs his attention or approval.
- Approve or decline what his daughter is trying to do on his behalf, clearly and simply.
- Keep control over his own accounts even while accepting help with the mechanics.
- Avoid being pressured or confused into approving something he doesn't understand.

**JTBD statements**
- When my daughter wants to help with a form, I want to understand in plain language what she's
  asking to do, so I can approve it with real understanding rather than blind trust.
- When a notice arrives that I can't easily read or understand, I want a plain-language summary
  and a clear next step, so I don't miss something important.
- When I no longer want someone's help, I want to be able to end that access simply, so my
  independence is never diminished by having accepted assistance once.

**Pain points**
- Dense, jargon-heavy notices from institutions that assume a level of literacy or digital comfort
  he doesn't have.
- Difficulty distinguishing a legitimate request for approval from something confusing or
  manipulative.
- No simple, low-friction way to grant or revoke help without a complicated flow.

**Golden flows touched.** 6 (Family assisted access), 5 (Tax notice/grievance), 3 (Nomination
readiness).

## 4. Parent or Guardian

**Role.** Managing records and services on behalf of a minor child or a dependent adult under their
guardianship.

**Profile sketch.** Priya Nair, 38, a homemaker and part-time consultant in Kochi, managing her
16-year-old son's emerging identity documents (a first PAN card, an upcoming Voter ID at 18) and
her own elderly mother-in-law's affairs under an informal but consistent family arrangement.

**Goals**
- Track document and identity milestones coming up for a dependant (turning 18, first PAN, first
  licence).
- Keep a dependant's records consistent as they transition from child-managed to self-managed.
- Manage requests on a dependant's behalf clearly and separately from her own profile.

**JTBD statements**
- When my son turns 18, I want to see exactly which new identity documents and registrations
  become relevant, so I can help him get them in the right order without missing one.
- When I act on behalf of a dependant, I want that activity clearly separated from my own
  profile, so nothing gets confused between our two identities.
- When guardianship or dependency status changes, I want the platform to reflect that change
  cleanly, so access doesn't linger past when it's appropriate.

**Pain points**
- No structured guidance on the sequence of identity milestones a minor approaches.
- Risk of a dependant's and guardian's own records blending together in ways that cause confusion
  later.
- Uncertainty about when guardian-level access should end and independent access should begin.

**Golden flows touched.** 1 (Unified onboarding), 6 (Family assisted access), 3 (Nomination
readiness).

## 5. Professional Representative (CA / lawyer / adviser)

**Role.** A Chartered Accountant, lawyer, or financial adviser handling specifically delegated
tasks for a client under an explicit permission tier — never implying power of attorney unless a
backing legal instrument exists.

**Profile sketch.** CA Meenal Kulkarni, 45, runs a small tax-and-compliance practice in Nashik
serving about 60 individual clients, several of whom delegate their annual tax-notice responses
and filings to her through the platform rather than emailing scanned documents back and forth.

**Goals**
- See exactly which clients have delegated exactly which tasks to her, with no ambiguity about
  scope.
- Prepare and submit specific filings or responses within her granted permission tier.
- Maintain a clean audit trail of what she did for each client and when, for her own liability
  protection.

**JTBD statements**
- When a client delegates a tax-notice response to me, I want to see the notice, prepare the
  reply, and submit it within my granted permission tier, so I can serve them without needing
  broader access than the task requires.
- When I complete a delegated task, I want a durable record of exactly what I submitted and on
  whose authorisation, so both my client and I are protected if a question arises later.
- When a client revokes my access, I want that revocation to take effect immediately and
  visibly, so there's no ambiguity about when my authority ended.

**Pain points**
- Today, client delegation happens informally over email or WhatsApp, with no audit trail and no
  clear scope boundary.
- No consistent way to prove, after the fact, exactly what she was authorised to do for a given
  client.
- Risk of being perceived as having broader authority (e.g., power of attorney) than she actually
  holds.

**Golden flows touched.** 5 (Tax notice/grievance), 6 (Family assisted access), 3 (Nomination
readiness).

## 6. Estate Planner

**Role.** A living person actively preparing their estate: organising documents, reviewing
readiness, and keeping nominations and beneficiary designations current.

**Profile sketch.** Suresh Iyer, 58, a business owner in Chennai, married with two adult children,
who has started thinking seriously about estate readiness after a close friend's sudden death left
that friend's family struggling for months to locate accounts and reconcile outdated nominations.

**Goals**
- Get a clear, honest readiness score for his estate — based on completeness, not on asset value.
- Identify outdated or missing nominations across every asset he holds.
- Keep a will, trust documents, and authority credentials referenced and current.
- Leave his family a usable emergency reference, without exposing sensitive detail prematurely.

**JTBD statements**
- When I review my estate readiness, I want a completeness score and a specific list of gaps
  (missing nominee, unregistered will, stale document), so I know exactly what to fix next.
- When I update a nomination on one account, I want to see every other account where the same
  nominee should probably be reviewed, so inconsistency doesn't creep back in.
- When I'm not available, I want my family to have clear, permission-appropriate emergency
  guidance, so they aren't starting from zero in a moment of crisis.

**Pain points**
- No existing way to see estate readiness as a single, trackable score rather than a vague sense
  of "I should get around to this."
- Nominations made years ago at different institutions have drifted out of sync with his current
  wishes and family situation.
- Reluctance to give family full visibility into finances while alive, but a real need for some
  emergency-relevant information to be accessible if something happens to him.

**Golden flows touched.** 3 (Nomination readiness), 7 (Post-death succession, preparatory half),
6 (Family assisted access).

## 7. Claimant / Legal Representative

**Role.** A person acting on a deceased person's estate after death — a nominee, beneficiary,
surviving joint holder, executor, administrator, or legal heir — as precisely distinguished in
`TERMINOLOGY.md`.

**Profile sketch.** Divya Reddy, 47, Suresh Iyer's daughter-in-law in the succession scenario,
acting as one of several legal heirs after his death, coordinating with her spouse and
sister-in-law to file claims across two banks, one insurer, and one investment platform, without a
will in place for one particular property.

**Goals**
- Get one consolidated view of everything her father-in-law held, before starting any individual
  claim.
- Understand which claims can proceed smoothly (clear nominee, joint holding) versus which require
  legal-heir determination.
- Reuse verified documents (death certificate, identity proof, relationship proof) across every
  institution instead of resubmitting them each time.
- Track every claim's status across every institution in one place during an already difficult
  period.

**JTBD statements**
- When I begin the claims process, I want to see every institution my father-in-law had a
  relationship with, so I don't miss an account or a policy in the confusion of the moment.
- When I've already submitted a verified document to one institution, I want to reuse it for the
  next claim rather than resubmitting from scratch, so the process is less exhausting for the
  family.
- When a claim requires legal-heir determination rather than a straightforward nominee payout, I
  want that distinction made clear early, so I know what additional legal step to expect.

**Pain points**
- No consolidated starting picture of what the deceased held, forcing reconstruction from paper
  records and guesswork.
- Repeated resubmission of the same evidence to every institution separately.
- Difficulty distinguishing, without professional help, which claims are procedurally simple and
  which require a legal-heir or succession-certificate process.

**Golden flows touched.** 7 (Post-death succession — all three sub-scenarios: prepared smooth
claim, no-will legal-heir claim, false-death correction), 6 (Family assisted access).

## 8. Government Service Officer

**Role.** An official at a government department or registrar's office processing citizen-initiated
requests and death/identity-related notifications.

**Profile sketch.** Anil Kumar, 39, a Registrar of Births and Deaths staff officer in a district
office in Madhya Pradesh, handling death registrations and responding to record-correction
requests, including occasional false-death disputes.

**Goals**
- Process incoming requests (registrations, corrections) with complete, correctly formatted
  information the first time.
- Verify evidence efficiently without an unmanageable volume of follow-up correspondence.
- Correct erroneous records (e.g., a false-death report) cleanly, with a clear audit trail.

**JTBD statements**
- When a death registration request arrives through the platform, I want it accompanied by
  correctly formatted, complete evidence, so I can process it without a deficiency round-trip.
- When a false-death correction is raised, I want a clear, auditable workflow to reverify and
  correct the record, so the citizen's status is restored without ambiguity lingering.
- When I need more information from a claimant, I want to request it and be notified the moment
  it's provided, so cases don't stall silently on my desk.

**Pain points**
- High proportion of incomplete or incorrectly formatted incoming applications today.
- No structured, auditable channel for a citizen to challenge and correct an erroneous
  deceased-status record.
- Manual correspondence for routine deficiency requests.

**Golden flows touched.** 8 (Institution ops console processing), 7 (Post-death succession,
false-death correction sub-scenario).

## 9. Financial Institution Operations Officer

**Role.** Staff at a bank, insurer, or investment platform processing service requests and claims
from citizens and claimants.

**Profile sketch.** Farah Sheikh, 33, a claims-processing officer at a mid-size private bank's
back office in Mumbai, handling nomination updates, address-change requests, and death-claim
settlements as part of her daily case queue.

**Goals**
- Work through a queue of service requests and claims with clear, complete supporting information.
- Apply maker-checker review consistently and efficiently.
- Flag deficiencies clearly to the citizen/claimant and track their resolution.
- Escalate suspicious or fraud-flagged cases appropriately without slowing down routine ones.

**JTBD statements**
- When a service request lands in my queue, I want it to arrive with all mandatory information and
  documents already validated against our requirements, so I can process it without an
  avoidable back-and-forth.
- When I need to raise a deficiency, I want to communicate it clearly and track the claimant's
  response in the same case, so nothing gets lost between separate emails or calls.
- When a case shows a fraud signal, I want it flagged and routed for review automatically, so
  routine cases aren't slowed down by manual triage.

**Pain points**
- High volume of incomplete applications requiring rework.
- Fragmented communication channels (phone, email, in-person) for the same case.
- Manual fraud-pattern detection across a high case volume.

**Golden flows touched.** 8 (Institution ops console processing), 7 (Post-death succession), 3
(Nomination readiness), 2 (Address-change).

## 10. Institution Administrator

**Role.** Configures and maintains an institution's service catalogue, integration settings, and
staff access within the platform.

**Profile sketch.** Vikram Chawla, 44, a digital-operations lead at a mid-size insurer's head
office in Gurugram, responsible for keeping the insurer's service catalogue, SLA targets, and
integration status current within Suvidha's institution console.

**Goals**
- Keep the institution's published services, required documents, and fees accurate and current.
- Monitor integration health (sandbox vs. active) and SLA adherence across service types.
- Manage which staff roles (maker, checker, verification officer) have which permissions.

**JTBD statements**
- When our service terms or document requirements change, I want to update the catalogue in one
  place, so every citizen-facing request reflects the current rules immediately.
- When an integration degrades or falls out of sync, I want to see that clearly, so I can
  intervene before it affects citizen-facing service quality.
- When I onboard new staff, I want to assign maker/checker/verification roles precisely, so
  case handling stays properly segregated.

**Pain points**
- No single admin view of catalogue accuracy, integration health, and SLA performance together.
- Staff role assignment handled ad hoc rather than through a consistent permission model.

**Golden flows touched.** 8 (Institution ops console processing).

citizens and claimants.

**Profile sketch.** Neha Joshi, 29, a verification officer at a depository participant's back
office in Ahmedabad, reviewing relationship proofs, identity documents, and death-event evidence
for claims routed to her queue.

**Goals**
- Verify submitted evidence efficiently against a consistent checklist.
- Flag inconclusive or suspicious evidence for further review rather than approving under
  uncertainty.
- Keep verification turnaround within SLA.

**JTBD statements**
- When evidence is submitted for a claim or a relationship, I want a consistent checklist to
  verify against, so my decisions are defensible and repeatable across cases.
- When evidence looks inconclusive or inconsistent, I want to route it for further review rather

## 12. Grievance Officer

**Role.** Handles citizen complaints and escalations that a routine service request or claim
process did not resolve satisfactorily.

**Profile sketch.** Ramesh Pillai, 51, a nodal grievance officer at a public-sector bank's regional
office in Bhopal, handling escalated complaints about delayed service requests, disputed nominee
changes, and SLA breaches referred up from front-line processing.

**Goals**
- See the full history of a case (request, deficiency, communication) before adjudicating a
  grievance, not just the complaint text.
- Resolve grievances within regulatory timeframes.
- Escalate appropriately to an ombudsman or higher authority when warranted.

**JTBD statements**
- When a grievance is raised, I want the complete underlying case history available immediately,
  so I can adjudicate fairly without reconstructing events from scratch.
- When a grievance needs escalation, I want a clear, trackable escalation path, so the citizen
  isn't left wondering whether anything is happening.
- When a grievance is resolved, I want the resolution and rationale recorded clearly, so it forms
  a defensible audit trail.

**Pain points**
- Grievances often arrive disconnected from the underlying case's full history.
- Inconsistent escalation pathways across different complaint types.

**Golden flows touched.** 5 (Tax notice/grievance orchestration), 8 (Institution ops console
processing).

## 13. Auditor / Regulator

**Role.** Reviews platform-wide activity for compliance, fraud patterns, SLA adherence, and
consent-handling correctness — internal audit or external regulatory oversight.

**Profile sketch.** Sunita Rao, 49, an internal auditor for a financial institution's compliance
function in Delhi, periodically reviewing maker-checker adherence, consent-revocation handling, and
fraud-signal resolution across the platform's case history.

**Goals**
- Verify that every sensitive action (approval, access grant, revocation) has a complete,
  immutable audit trail.
- Confirm maker-checker segregation was actually followed, not just nominally configured.
- Identify patterns suggesting systemic gaps (repeated SLA breaches, unresolved fraud signals).

**JTBD statements**
- When I review a sample of approved claims, I want to see the complete maker-checker decision
  trail for each one, so I can confirm segregation of duties was genuinely enforced.
- When a consent grant is revoked, I want to verify that access actually stopped at that moment,
  so I can confirm the platform's access controls are trustworthy in practice, not just on paper.
- When I look for systemic risk, I want aggregate visibility into fraud-signal resolution and
  SLA adherence across institutions, so I can flag patterns before they become larger failures.

**Pain points**
- Audit trails scattered across different systems' own logs rather than a single, append-only,
  cross-domain record.
- Difficulty confirming, after the fact, that a permission was actually enforced at the moment it
  mattered, not just correctly configured.

**Golden flows touched.** 8 (Institution ops console processing), 7 (Post-death succession), 6
(Family assisted access).
