# Access Control Matrix

A full role × capability matrix for every persona across both product domains. This complements
`docs/SECURITY.md` §3 (RBAC/ABAC design) and `docs/PRIVACY.md` §5 (progressive disclosure) — those
documents explain the *mechanism*; this document is the *reference table* an implementer or
reviewer checks a specific role against.

**Reading the table:** "Own data" means data about the person's own record (their own `Person`,
`EstatePlan`, `ServiceRequest`, etc.). "Others' data" always names the specific condition that must
hold — no role sees others' data unconditionally. "Financial balances" specifically means exact
figures, as distinct from the banded/masked values (`amountBand`, `maskedAccountNumber`) that a
broader set of roles may see as part of normal claim/asset context — the matrix calls out where
even the *banded* value is withheld, and separately where an *exact* figure is available at all
(normally: never, outside the account holder's own view and the institution's internal settlement
system).

## 1. Legacy & Succession domain personas

| Persona | Views own data | Views others' data (condition) | Can edit | Can submit requests | Can approve/decide | Financial balances (condition) | Can see audit logs | Special constraints |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Estate Planner** | Yes — full | No | Own `EstatePlan`, `Asset`, `Nomination`, `WillRecord`, `TrustRecord`, `TrustedContact` grants | N/A pre-death (planning only) | No | Own, exact (it's their own asset) | No — own actions only, via own activity view, not raw `AuditEvent` | Never sees anyone else's plan; readiness score is completeness-based, never a wealth signal |
| **Trusted Contact** | Yes — own identity/profile | Only the grantor's data explicitly scoped by an `active` `AccessGrant`/`AccessPolicy`, and only after the grant is activated | No edit rights over the grantor's assets/plan — view-only per policy scope | Can initiate a claim (becomes a `Claimant`) only once death is verified and they onboard via the invited path | No | Never — no `AccessPolicy.visibilityLevel` includes balances | No | Access is strictly bounded by the named policy; never login as the grantor; never treated as nominee/executor/owner by virtue of this role alone |
| **Nominee** | Yes — own identity | The specific asset(s) they are nominated on, once death is verified and their claim is confirmed | No | Yes — files a claim as `Claimant` (`claimedRole = nominee`) | No (institution decides) | Own claimed asset only, once confirmed; banded until settlement, exact only for their own payout amount | No | Payout is "subject to applicable rights" — never presented as final legal ownership |
| **Beneficiary** | Yes — own identity | The specific policy/will/trust asset they are named under, once confirmed | No | Yes — files a claim | No | Own designated share only, once confirmed | No | Same "subject to applicable rights" framing as Nominee; share percentage from `BeneficiaryDesignation` is advisory until institution/legal confirmation |
| **Surviving Joint Holder** | Yes — own identity and their joint holding | The joint asset itself (already had operating visibility pre-death) | Can operate the account per mandate (pre-existing right, not new) | Yes — files a claim/notification for ownership update | No | Yes, on the jointly held asset — this is continuation of pre-existing access, not new disclosure | No | Visibility here pre-dates the death event; nothing new is disclosed to them that they didn't already have as a joint operator |
| **Executor** | Yes — own identity | Estate-wide, once appointment is verified against a valid `WillRecord`/probate | Can direct distribution per the will (legal act, not a Suvidha-executed transfer) | Yes — can act across multiple `Claim`s for the estate | No (institution decides; executor authorises/directs, does not adjudicate) | Estate-wide, once confirmed, subject to progressive reveal per asset as each institution confirms | No | Confirmed only against will authenticity/registration and no superseding later will; a disputed will blocks estate-wide access until resolved |
| **Administrator** | Yes — own identity | Estate-wide, once court-appointed (letters of administration verified) | Same estate-administration powers as Executor, sourced from court appointment rather than a will | Yes | No | Estate-wide, once confirmed | No | Appointment must be verified as a court/competent-authority act, not self-asserted |
| **Legal Heir** | Yes — own identity | Only the specific asset(s) claimed, and only once legal-heir/succession documentation is verified | No | Yes — files a claim, typically alongside other heirs | No | Own share, once confirmed; multi-heir cases always route through human review regardless of value | No | Never sees co-heirs' full personal data beyond what's needed to coordinate a shared claim (e.g. NOC) |
| **Guardian** (acting for a minor claimant) | Yes — own identity | Only the minor dependant's specific claimed asset, once guardian documentation is verified | Can act on the minor's behalf for the specific claim only | Yes, on behalf of the minor | No | The minor's claimed share, once confirmed, typically routed to a restricted guardian/minor account | No | The minor is never made to authenticate or transact directly; guardian documentation is mandatory regardless of claim value |
| **Claimant** (umbrella role — see specific roles above) | Yes — own identity and verification status | Progressive — see `docs/PRIVACY.md` §5 table | No, until role-specific rights apply above | Yes | No | Progressive, per §5 of `docs/PRIVACY.md` | No | `wasPreAuthorised` (invited Trusted Contact) vs. not (uninvited family member) changes onboarding rigor, not final disclosure ceiling |
| **Registrar (of Births and Deaths)** | N/A (institution role) | Death-event and evidence records within their jurisdiction | Can update `DeathEvent`/`DeathEventEvidence`/`DeathEventCorrection` status fields relevant to registration/correction | N/A | Verifies/corrects registration status (not a claims decision) | No — registrar function has no reason to see financial balances | Own jurisdiction's registration actions, via reporting, not raw cross-domain `AuditEvent` | Never sees claim/asset detail — registrar scope is birth/death registration and correction only |
| **Institution Claims / Verification Officer** | N/A (institution role) | Claims and death-event matches for their own institution only (`institutionId`-scoped) | Can update `DocumentVerification`, `DeathEventMatch.status`, raise `DeficiencyRequest` | N/A (receives, does not submit citizen requests) | Records `Decision` as Maker or as `verification_officer` scope, not final institution decision alone | Institution's own account data for the claim under review, banded/exact per institution's own internal policy — not a Suvidha-imposed limit on the institution's own systems | Own institution's actions, for internal QA | Strict tenant isolation — never another institution's claims (`docs/SECURITY.md` §9) |
| **Maker** | N/A | Claims assigned to them (`CaseAssignment.role = maker`) at their own institution | Records own `Decision` (`recommend_approve`/`recommend_reject`/etc.) | N/A | Recommends only — cannot finalise alone | Per institution's own claim-review context | No | Cannot also act as Checker on the same claim |
| **Checker** | N/A | Claims where a Maker decision exists, at their own institution | Records own `Decision`, independent of Maker's | N/A | Concurs, rejects, or refers to Adjudicator | Per institution's own claim-review context | No | Must independently review evidence, not merely ratify the Maker |
| **Adjudicator** | N/A | Escalated/disputed/high-value claims at their own institution | Records final `Decision` | N/A | Final decision authority for escalated cases | Per institution's own claim-review context | No | Only reached via Maker/Checker escalation or institution-threshold rule, never a default first reviewer |
| **Grievance Officer** | N/A | `Grievance`/`Escalation`/`Appeal` records raised against their institution | Updates `Grievance.status`, `Escalation`, `Appeal` | N/A | Resolves grievances; does not decide underlying claims | Only as needed to investigate the specific grievance, scoped to that case | Full access to the audit trail **for the case under grievance**, to investigate what happened | Deliberately separate from claim Maker/Checker/Adjudicator — investigates process fairness, not claim merits |
| **Auditor** | N/A | Full read access to `AuditEvent` and, for oversight purposes, underlying entities referenced by it | No edit rights anywhere | N/A | No approve/decide rights | Read-only, only as needed for a specific audit/investigation, not a standing dashboard of citizen balances | Yes — full, this is the role's defining capability | Oversight only; never approves, rejects, or edits a claim; access itself should be logged |
| **Integration Administrator** | N/A | Connector/Integration configuration and status, not claim/citizen content | Can configure `Integration.status`, `Connector` metadata, activate `RuleVersion`s | N/A | Can activate a `RuleVersion` (a rule *change*, not a claim decision) | No | Own configuration-change actions | Should require a second approver for rule-version activation in a production deployment (see `docs/SECURITY.md` §7) |

## 2. Lifelong citizen administration domain personas

| Persona | Views own data | Views others' data (condition) | Can edit | Can submit requests | Can approve/decide | Financial balances (condition) | Can see audit logs | Special constraints |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Independent Citizen** | Yes — full `CitizenProfile`, `InstitutionRelationship`, `ServiceRequest`, `LifeEvent` | No | Own profile fields, own service requests, own consent scopes | Yes | Self-approves own submissions only (not an institution decision) | Own, exact | No — own activity view only | Manages own delegated access to others (Family Administrator/Professional Representative), not the reverse |
| **Family Administrator** | Yes — own identity | The specific Assisted Citizen's data, only within the scope of a `DelegatedTask`'s `permissionTier` | Only actions the `permissionTier` allows (`view`/`assist`/`prepare`/`submit`/`sign`/`receive_communication`) | Only where `permissionTier` includes `permission_to_submit` | No — never treated as legal authority regardless of tier | Only if explicitly delegated and only to the extent the tier and the Assisted Citizen's own consent scope (`ConsentScope`) allows; never assumed | No | Distinct from Trusted Contact (Legacy & Succession-specific) and from Professional Representative; permission tier is never legal authority or ownership |
| **Assisted Citizen** | Yes — full, same as Independent Citizen | No | Own data, though may choose to delegate specific actions | Yes, or via a Family Administrator/Professional Representative acting on a `DelegatedTask` | Retains final approval unless explicitly delegated a `permission_to_sign`/`permission_to_submit` task, which still requires `pending_owner_approval → approved` for material actions | Own, exact | No | The person being assisted always remains the rights-holder; delegation never transfers ownership of the relationship |
| **Parent / Guardian** (lifelong-administration context) | Yes — own identity | The minor/dependant's `CitizenProfile`, `ServiceRequest`s, and institutional relationships they manage on the dependant's behalf | Yes, for the dependant's records, until the dependant reaches majority/independence | Yes, on the dependant's behalf | No independent institutional decision authority — guardianship is a legal fact the institution separately verifies | Dependant's own, exact, since this is a legitimate guardianship function, not third-party disclosure | No | Guardianship status itself should be periodically re-verified, especially as a dependant approaches majority |
| **Professional Representative** (CA, lawyer, adviser) | Yes — own identity | Only the specific Principal's data explicitly covered by a `DelegatedTask`, under an active, non-`revoked` `ProfessionalRepresentative` relationship | Only what the `permissionTier` allows | Only where `permissionTier` includes `permission_to_submit` | No — a `permission_to_sign` tier is still not power of attorney unless backed by an actual `CourtOrder`/`AuthorityCredential` record | Only within the specific delegated scope, per the Principal's own consent | No | Never implies power of attorney absent a backing legal instrument (per `docs/TERMINOLOGY.md` §4); revocation (`ProfessionalRepresentative.status = revoked`) is immediate |
| **Government Service Officer** | N/A | Service requests/records within their own government service's scope | Updates official status on their own service's `ServiceDefinition`/`Application` records | N/A | Approves/rejects within their own service's jurisdiction only | No reason to access financial balances outside their service's own domain (e.g. a passport officer has no view into bank data) | Own service's actions | Scoped strictly to their institution/service — no cross-institution visibility |
| **Financial Institution Operations Officer** | N/A | Financial `InstitutionRelationship`/`ServiceRequest`/claim data for their own institution only | Updates their institution's own records/status | N/A | Approves/rejects within their own institution's threshold and policy | Institution's own account data for the citizen relationship under review | Own institution's actions | Same tenant-isolation constraint as Institution Claims/Verification Officer in the Legacy & Succession table above |
| **Institution Administrator** | N/A | Configuration and aggregate/reporting data for their own institution; not individual citizen content beyond what a specific case requires | Configures `ServiceCatalogue`, `ServiceDefinition`, SLA targets, connector settings for their institution | N/A | Not a per-case approver; administers the service catalogue and policy parameters cases are decided against | No standing access; only as needed to investigate a specific escalation | Own institution's configuration-change actions | Distinct from Government Service Officer/Financial Institution Operations Officer — administers the system, doesn't process individual cases |

## 3. Cross-cutting rules that apply regardless of role

1. **No role, anywhere, can log in as another person.** Every capability above is exercised under
   the actor's own verified identity, with delegated/representative capability layered on top via
   `DelegatedTask`/`AccessGrant`/`ProfessionalRepresentative`, never via shared or borrowed
   credentials.
2. **Financial balances default to banded, not exact,** for any viewer who is not the account
   holder themself, a confirmed claimant on that specific asset, or the institution's own internal
   settlement process. The "condition" column above states the specific narrow exception in each
   row; absent that condition, the default is banded-or-nothing.
3. **Audit-log visibility is deliberately scarce.** Only the Auditor role has standing, broad audit
   access; a Grievance Officer's audit access is scoped to the specific case under investigation;
   every other role sees, at most, their own activity history, not the platform-wide `AuditEvent`
   stream.
4. **Approval authority never collapses to a single actor** for claim decisions — Maker, Checker,
   and (where escalated) Adjudicator are structurally distinct, and no citizen-facing role (Nominee,
   Beneficiary, Executor, etc.) ever holds approval authority over their own claim.
5. **A permission tier or access grant is never legal authority.** `DelegatedTask.permissionTier`,
   `TrustedContact`/`AccessGrant`, and `ProfessionalRepresentative` status are all platform-level
   access constructs; none of them substitute for a will, nomination, trust, survivorship mandate,
   succession certificate, probate, letters of administration, legal-heir certificate, or
   institution-specific legal requirement.
